Our Mission

Strategic Framework

Awdal Water & Education Initiative Inc. (AWEI) is an independent international non-governmental organization dedicated to mitigating the devastating impacts of climate-induced drought in Somaliland. By building resilient water infrastructure and stabilizing local primary schools, we safeguard both community health and the educational future of the region's children.

The Challenge

The Crisis: Water Scarcity & Educational Disruption

Understanding the root causes that drive AWEI's mission across northern Somalia.

Regional Context

A Cycle We Can Break

Extended ecological crises in northern Somalia (Somaliland) have disproportionately impacted rural and pastoralist families. When traditional water sources fail, children — particularly young girls — are pulled out of classrooms to walk miles in search of water, or schools are forced to shut down entirely due to unsafe sanitary conditions.

AWEI breaks this cycle by anchoring permanent, clean water infrastructure directly into foundational educational hubs — creating a stabilizing force that protects both community health and children's futures simultaneously.

Traditional shallow wells fail completely during multi-season droughts, leaving deep-aquifer borehole drilling as the only viable solution to keep communities and schools anchored in place. Without this infrastructure, displacement is inevitable.

Why This Matters
🏫 Schools forced to close without clean water access
👧 Girls disproportionately pulled from classrooms
🌵 Multi-season droughts intensifying across the region
💧 Shallow wells unreliable — deep boreholes are the solution
